
# 🧹 Disability-Based Harassment in U.S. Public Schools — Data Analysis

> Cleaned and analyzed U.S. government data on disability-based harassment 
> in public schools across 52 states (2017-18)

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project involves cleaning and analyzing a real-world dataset from the 
**U.S. Department of Education Office for Civil Rights (2017-18 Civil Rights Data Collection)**.

The dataset tracks students disciplined for harassment or bullying on the basis 
of disability across all 50 U.S. states, the District of Columbia, and Puerto Rico.

---

## 🔍 What I Analyzed

- 📊 Total students disciplined per state
- 👦👧 Gender breakdown — Male vs Female cases
- 🌍 Racial/ethnic distribution of disciplined students
- ♿ Impact on students with disabilities (IDEA & Section 504)
- 🏫 School reporting rates by state

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning Steps

- Removed inconsistent formatting and messy headers
- Standardized percentage values across all columns
- Handled missing and zero-value entries
- Separated data across Total, Male, and Female sheets for clearer analysis
- Ensured all state entries were complete and correctly labeled

---

## 📈 Key Findings

- **12,214** students were disciplined nationally for disability-based harassment
- **White students** made up the largest group at ~48.7% of total cases
- **Black/African American students** were disproportionately represented at ~21.9%
- **Male students (9,494)** accounted for ~78% of all disciplined cases
- **Students with disabilities under IDEA** made up ~24% of cases nationally
- States like **California, New Jersey, and Oregon** reported the highest case counts
- **99.2%** of schools participated in reporting
